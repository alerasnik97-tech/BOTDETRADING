# MANIPULANTE 3.0 — HYPOTHESIS TREE

## Primary Hypothesis (Core Thesis)
In **EURUSD**, after the price executes a verifiable sweep of relevant higher-timeframe (HTF) liquidity, a programmatic statistical advantage (edge) exists if the lower-timeframe (LTF) entry is triggered upon an objective structural shift confirmation, provided that:
- The sweep occurs directly over a defined, relevant institutional reference level.
- The prevailing HTF directional bias does not contradict the intended transaction.
- The LTF entry signal generation and fill logic are strictly causal (zero look-ahead bias).
- The designated Stop Loss represents real structural or extreme invalidation.
- The operational window enforces fail-close macroeconomic news exclusion filters.
- All attribution metrics are calculated net of realistic institutional friction ($5/lot commissions and slippage stress).

## Granular Sub-Hypotheses (Search Dimensions)
- **H1 (Level Dependency):** The validity of the edge depends strongly on the specific class of HTF liquidity level swept (e.g., Daily Extremes vs. Session Extremes vs. Swing Extremes).
- **H2 (Directional Concordance):** The edge requires explicit alignment with broader HTF structure or momentum filters (Premium/Discount positioning or Moving Average trend filters).
- **H3 (Confirmation Strictness):** The edge relies on the specific structural signature of the LTF confirmation (Simple CHOCH vs. CHOCH with volumetric displacement vs. CHOCH mapping a Fair Value Gap).
- **H4 (Entry Execution Mode):** The edge varies based on the execution mechanism deployed at confirmation (Market entry on close vs. Breakout stop entry vs. Limit retracement entry inside FVG).
- **H5 (Risk Management Optimization):** The edge is highly sensitive to Stop Loss logic (Sweep extreme vs. Structure extreme) and dynamic target/break-even mechanics.
- **H6 (Temporal Concentration):** The programmatic edge is concentrated within highly liquid session windows (e.g., NY Open 07:00–11:00 vs. London Overlap).
- **H7 (Volatility Regime Conditioning):** Strategy alpha is modulated by macro volatility regimes (e.g., ATR expansion phases).
- **H8 (Slippage Fragility):** The hypothetical edge is a low-latency execution artifact that collapses entirely when subjected to realistic stress slippage (0.1 to 0.5 pips).
- **H9 (Outlier Attribution):** Net profitability is heavily skewed by a tiny subset of fat-tailed distribution events rather than exhibiting robust high-frequency consistency.
- **H10 (Null Hypothesis):** The discretionary premise cannot be translated into a reliable, programmatic execution model with statistical significance net of execution costs.
